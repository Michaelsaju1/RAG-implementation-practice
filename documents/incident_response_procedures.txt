DOCUMENT: Security Incident Response Procedures
ORGANIZATION: Defense Information Systems Division
EFFECTIVE DATE: February 20, 2025
CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

1. PURPOSE

This document establishes the procedures for identifying, reporting, and
responding to security incidents involving classified information, controlled
unclassified information (CUI), and information systems within the Defense
Information Systems Division (DISD).

2. DEFINITIONS

2.1 Security Incident — Any event that involves the actual or possible
compromise, loss, or unauthorized disclosure of classified information or CUI.

2.2 Data Breach — Unauthorized access to, or disclosure of, personally
identifiable information (PII) or other protected data.

2.3 Cyber Incident — An action taken through the use of computer networks
that results in a compromise or an effect on an information system or the
information residing therein.

2.4 Spillage — The transfer of classified information to an information
system not accredited for that classification level.

3. INCIDENT CATEGORIES

3.1 Category 1 — CRITICAL
Confirmed compromise of TOP SECRET or SCI information, or active intrusion
on classified networks. Response time: IMMEDIATE (within 15 minutes).

3.2 Category 2 — HIGH
Confirmed compromise of SECRET information, confirmed data breach affecting
more than 1000 records, or successful intrusion on unclassified networks.
Response time: within 1 hour.

3.3 Category 3 — MODERATE
Compromise of CONFIDENTIAL information, spillage from classified to
unclassified systems, loss of removable media containing classified data,
or suspected but unconfirmed intrusion. Response time: within 4 hours.

3.4 Category 4 — LOW
Policy violations with no confirmed compromise, unsuccessful intrusion
attempts, loss of CUI, or minor procedural deviations. Response time:
within 24 hours.

4. REPORTING PROCEDURES

4.1 Initial Discovery
Upon discovering or suspecting a security incident, any individual must:
    a) Stop the activity that may be causing or worsening the incident
    b) Do NOT attempt to assess the scope or remediate independently
    c) Preserve all evidence — do not power off systems, delete files, or
       disconnect network cables unless directed by security personnel
    d) Immediately notify their supervisor and the Information System
       Security Officer (ISSO)

4.2 ISSO Responsibilities
Upon receiving an incident report, the ISSO must:
    a) Assign an incident category based on Section 3
    b) Notify the Information System Security Manager (ISSM)
    c) For Category 1 or 2 incidents, immediately notify the Division Chief
       and the servicing counterintelligence office
    d) Initiate an incident log documenting all actions taken
    e) Activate the Incident Response Team (IRT) if warranted

4.3 External Notifications
    a) All cyber incidents must be reported to US-CERT within 1 hour of
       determination for Category 1 incidents, and within 72 hours for
       all other categories
    b) Data breaches involving PII must be reported to the Privacy Office
       within 24 hours
    c) Compromises of classified information must be reported to the
       cognizant Original Classification Authority (OCA)

5. INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURES

5.1 Containment
    a) Isolate affected systems from the network if active compromise is
       suspected — do this by physically disconnecting the network cable,
       NOT by powering off the system
    b) Revoke credentials of compromised accounts
    c) Block identified malicious IP addresses at the network perimeter
    d) Quarantine affected removable media

5.2 Assessment
    a) The IRT will conduct a preliminary damage assessment within 24 hours
    b) Determine the scope: what data was exposed, how many systems affected,
       what classification level was involved
    c) Identify the attack vector or cause of the incident
    d) Preserve forensic evidence following chain-of-custody procedures

5.3 Remediation
    a) Reimage affected systems if malware was involved
    b) Patch vulnerabilities that were exploited
    c) Reset all credentials that may have been compromised
    d) For spillage: sanitize the contaminated system in accordance with
       NSA/CSS EPL-02-001 procedures or arrange for system destruction
    e) Conduct a review of access controls to prevent recurrence

5.4 Recovery
    a) Restore affected systems from clean backups after verification
    b) Reconnect systems to the network only after IRT clearance
    c) Monitor recovered systems for 30 days for signs of residual compromise
    d) Restore user access incrementally with enhanced monitoring

6. DATA BREACH SPECIFIC PROCEDURES

6.1 Upon confirmation of a data breach involving PII:
    a) Determine the number of individuals affected
    b) Identify the specific data elements exposed (SSN, financial data, etc.)
    c) Assess the risk of harm to affected individuals
    d) Prepare notification letters in coordination with the Privacy Office
    e) Offer credit monitoring services when SSNs or financial data are exposed
    f) Report to Congress if more than 10,000 individuals are affected

7. POST-INCIDENT ACTIVITIES

7.1 After-Action Report
An after-action report must be completed within 30 days of incident closure.
The report must include:
    a) Timeline of events
    b) Root cause analysis
    c) Effectiveness of the response
    d) Lessons learned
    e) Recommended corrective actions

7.2 Corrective Actions
    a) All corrective actions must be assigned to responsible parties with
       completion deadlines
    b) The ISSM must track corrective actions to completion
    c) Systemic issues must be elevated to the Division Chief for resource
       allocation

7.3 Training Updates
    a) Incident response procedures must be updated based on lessons learned
    b) Personnel must be retrained on updated procedures within 60 days
    c) Tabletop exercises incorporating the new scenario must be conducted
       within 90 days
