DOCUMENT: Data Classification Policy
ORGANIZATION: Defense Information Systems Division
EFFECTIVE DATE: January 15, 2025
CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes the data classification framework for all information
handled by the Defense Information Systems Division (DISD). All personnel must
understand and apply these classification levels to protect national security
information appropriately.

2. CLASSIFICATION LEVELS

2.1 UNCLASSIFIED
Information that has been reviewed and determined not to require protection
against unauthorized disclosure. Unclassified information may still be subject
to access controls under other policies (e.g., Privacy Act, FOUO).

2.2 CONTROLLED UNCLASSIFIED INFORMATION (CUI)
Unclassified information that requires safeguarding or dissemination controls
pursuant to law, regulation, or government-wide policy. CUI replaces the
former FOUO, SBU, and LES markings.

2.3 CONFIDENTIAL
Information whose unauthorized disclosure could reasonably be expected to cause
DAMAGE to national security. Examples include technical specifications for
non-critical defense systems, routine diplomatic communications, and
operational planning data for non-sensitive exercises.

2.4 SECRET
Information whose unauthorized disclosure could reasonably be expected to cause
SERIOUS DAMAGE to national security. Examples include detailed intelligence
reports, weapons system performance parameters, war planning documents, and
critical infrastructure vulnerability assessments.

2.5 TOP SECRET
Information whose unauthorized disclosure could reasonably be expected to cause
EXCEPTIONALLY GRAVE DAMAGE to national security. Examples include strategic
nuclear operations plans, human intelligence source identities, and advanced
cryptographic algorithms used in national defense.

3. MARKING REQUIREMENTS

3.1 All documents must display the overall classification level in the header
and footer of every page.

3.2 Portion markings are required for CONFIDENTIAL and above. Each paragraph,
figure, table, and appendix must be individually marked with its classification
level using standard abbreviations: (U), (CUI), (C), (S), (TS).

3.3 Derivative classification decisions must reference the source material's
classification guide or the original classification authority.

4. HANDLING REQUIREMENTS

4.1 UNCLASSIFIED and CUI information may be stored on authorized government
information systems that meet NIST SP 800-171 requirements.

4.2 CONFIDENTIAL information must be stored in GSA-approved security containers
when not in use and processed only on systems accredited at the CONFIDENTIAL
level or higher.

4.3 SECRET information must be stored in GSA-approved security containers with
supplemental controls. Access requires a valid SECRET clearance and a
demonstrated need-to-know. SECRET materials must be processed on SIPRNet or
equivalent accredited networks.

4.4 TOP SECRET information must be stored in approved vaults or secure
compartmented information facilities (SCIFs). Access requires a valid TOP
SECRET clearance and specific access approval. TOP SECRET materials must be
processed on JWICS or equivalent accredited networks.

5. DECLASSIFICATION

5.1 All classified documents must include a declassification date or event,
not to exceed 25 years from the date of original classification unless the
information falls under specific exemption categories.

5.2 Automatic declassification reviews are conducted at the 25-year mark in
accordance with Executive Order 13526.

6. PENALTIES

Unauthorized disclosure of classified information may result in administrative
sanctions, loss of security clearance, criminal prosecution, or all of the
above, depending on the severity and circumstances of the disclosure.
