DOCUMENT: Personnel Security and Training Requirements
ORGANIZATION: Defense Information Systems Division
EFFECTIVE DATE: January 30, 2025
CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

1. PURPOSE

This document establishes the security training and personnel vetting
requirements for all individuals who access Defense Information Systems
Division (DISD) information, facilities, or systems.

2. CLEARANCE REQUIREMENTS

2.1 All personnel must hold an active security clearance appropriate to the
highest level of classified information they will access.

2.2 Clearance levels and investigation requirements:
    a) CONFIDENTIAL — requires a National Agency Check with Law and Credit
       (NACLC) investigation, reinvestigated every 15 years.
    b) SECRET — requires a Tier 3 (T3) investigation, reinvestigated every
       10 years.
    c) TOP SECRET — requires a Tier 5 (T5) Single Scope Background
       Investigation (SSBI), reinvestigated every 5 years via continuous
       evaluation or periodic reinvestigation.

2.3 Access to Sensitive Compartmented Information (SCI) requires TOP SECRET
clearance plus additional approval from the cognizant Special Security Officer
(SSO) and indoctrination into the specific compartment.

3. MANDATORY TRAINING

3.1 Initial Security Orientation
All personnel must complete an initial security orientation within 5 business
days of being granted access to DISD facilities or systems. This orientation
covers:
    a) Classification levels and handling procedures
    b) Physical security requirements
    c) Information system security basics
    d) Reporting obligations
    e) Penalties for security violations

3.2 Annual Security Refresher Training
All cleared personnel must complete annual security refresher training. This
training must be completed by September 30 of each fiscal year. Topics include:
    a) Updates to security policies and procedures
    b) Current threat briefings
    c) Social engineering awareness
    d) Insider threat indicators
    e) Foreign travel reporting requirements

3.3 Classified Information Handling Training
Personnel who create, process, or transmit classified information must
complete a 4-hour classified information handling course before being
authorized to handle classified material independently. Topics include:
    a) Proper marking and portion marking
    b) Storage and access control requirements
    c) Transmission procedures for each classification level
    d) Destruction and sanitization procedures
    e) Derivative classification responsibilities

3.4 Cyber Awareness Training
All personnel with access to DISD information systems must complete the
DoD Cyber Awareness Challenge annually. Additionally, personnel in IT
security roles must complete:
    a) Role-based cybersecurity training (40 hours annually)
    b) Incident response procedures training (quarterly)
    c) Vulnerability assessment methodology (annually)

3.5 Insider Threat Awareness Training
All personnel must complete insider threat awareness training within 30 days
of initial access and annually thereafter. This training covers:
    a) Recognizing potential insider threat indicators
    b) Reporting procedures for concerning behavior
    c) The role of user activity monitoring
    d) Case studies of actual insider threat incidents

3.6 Removable Media Awareness Training
All personnel must complete removable media awareness training annually.
See the Removable Media and Portable Device Policy for details.

4. SPECIAL ACCESS TRAINING

4.1 Personnel accessing SCI must complete SCI indoctrination training specific
to each compartment they are read into.

4.2 Personnel serving as Original Classification Authorities (OCAs) must
complete OCA training before exercising classification authority and every
year thereafter.

4.3 Personnel responsible for derivative classification must complete
derivative classification training before performing derivative classification
and every two years thereafter.

5. FOREIGN TRAVEL

5.1 All cleared personnel must report planned foreign travel to their Security
Manager at least 30 days in advance.

5.2 A foreign travel security briefing is required before departure and a
debriefing is required within 5 business days of return.

5.3 Personnel with TOP SECRET/SCI access may be subject to additional travel
restrictions and must obtain approval from the SSO.

6. CONTINUOUS EVALUATION

6.1 All cleared personnel are subject to continuous evaluation (CE), which
may include automated checks of criminal records, financial records, and
public records.

6.2 Personnel must self-report life events that may affect their clearance
eligibility, including but not limited to: arrests, financial difficulties,
foreign contacts, mental health treatment, and substance abuse issues.

7. TRAINING RECORDS

7.1 All training completions must be documented in the Security Training
Management System (STMS).

7.2 Supervisors are responsible for ensuring their personnel complete all
required training by the established deadlines.

7.3 Failure to complete mandatory training by the deadline will result in
suspension of system access until training is completed.
