DOCUMENT: Removable Media and Portable Device Policy
ORGANIZATION: Defense Information Systems Division
EFFECTIVE DATE: March 1, 2025
CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

1. PURPOSE

This policy governs the use of removable media and portable electronic devices
within Defense Information Systems Division (DISD) facilities and on DISD
information systems. The improper use of removable media represents one of the
most significant vectors for data exfiltration and malware introduction.

2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all removable media including but not limited to:
USB flash drives, external hard drives, SD cards, optical discs (CD/DVD/Blu-ray),
magnetic tapes, and any device capable of storing or transferring digital data.

3. GENERAL PROHIBITIONS

3.1 The use of personally owned removable media on any government information
system is strictly PROHIBITED.

3.2 The connection of any removable media to classified networks (SIPRNet,
JWICS) is PROHIBITED unless explicitly authorized by the Information System
Security Manager (ISSM) and documented in the system's security plan.

3.3 Removable media used on classified systems must never be connected to
unclassified systems. Cross-domain transfer of data requires use of approved
cross-domain solutions and formal authorization.

4. AUTHORIZED USE

4.1 Government-furnished removable media may be used on unclassified networks
for legitimate business purposes with supervisor approval.

4.2 Use of removable media on classified systems requires:
    a) Written approval from the ISSM
    b) Media must be government-furnished and pre-scanned
    c) Media must be marked with the highest classification level of data
       it has ever contained
    d) All transfers must be logged in the media accountability system
    e) Media must be stored in an approved security container when not in use

4.3 Authorized removable media must be encrypted using FIPS 140-2 validated
encryption modules for any data classified as CUI or above.

5. MEDIA SANITIZATION AND DESTRUCTION

5.1 Removable media that has contained UNCLASSIFIED or CUI data may be
sanitized using NIST SP 800-88 guidelines and reused.

5.2 Removable media that has contained CONFIDENTIAL or SECRET data must be
degaussed (for magnetic media) or physically destroyed using approved methods.
Sanitization alone is not sufficient.

5.3 Removable media that has contained TOP SECRET data must be physically
destroyed by pulverizing, incinerating, or shredding to particles no larger
than 2mm. Destruction must be witnessed by two authorized individuals and
documented on a destruction certificate.

5.4 All destruction activities must be logged and reported to the security
office within 24 hours.

6. INCIDENT REPORTING

6.1 Any unauthorized use of removable media must be reported to the Information
System Security Officer (ISSO) within one hour of discovery.

6.2 Loss or theft of removable media that has contained classified information
constitutes a security incident and must be reported immediately through the
chain of command and to the servicing counterintelligence office.

6.3 Discovery of unapproved removable media connected to any system must be
treated as a potential compromise. The device must not be removed — the ISSO
must be contacted to coordinate forensic analysis.

7. TRAINING

All personnel with access to DISD information systems must complete removable
media awareness training annually. This training covers proper handling,
labeling, storage, and destruction procedures.
