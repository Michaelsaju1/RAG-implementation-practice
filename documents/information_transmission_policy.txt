DOCUMENT: Classified Information Transmission Policy
ORGANIZATION: Defense Information Systems Division
EFFECTIVE DATE: February 10, 2025
CLASSIFICATION: UNCLASSIFIED // FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes procedures for the secure transmission of classified
and controlled unclassified information (CUI) within and between Defense
Information Systems Division (DISD) facilities.

2. ELECTRONIC TRANSMISSION

2.1 UNCLASSIFIED / CUI
CUI may be transmitted via government email systems that employ TLS 1.2 or
higher encryption. When transmitting CUI externally, it must be encrypted
using a FIPS 140-2 validated module. Approved methods include S/MIME encrypted
email or transfer via approved secure file sharing platforms.

2.2 CONFIDENTIAL
CONFIDENTIAL information may be transmitted electronically only over networks
accredited at the CONFIDENTIAL level or higher (e.g., SIPRNet). Transmission
over unclassified networks is prohibited unless the data is encrypted using
NSA-approved Type 1 encryption.

2.3 SECRET
SECRET information must be transmitted electronically only via SIPRNet or
other networks accredited at the SECRET level or higher. The use of
NSA-approved Type 1 encryption is required for all SECRET transmissions that
traverse any network segment not fully accredited at the SECRET level.

2.4 TOP SECRET
TOP SECRET information must be transmitted electronically only via JWICS or
other networks accredited at the TOP SECRET level. No exceptions are permitted
for transmission over lower-accredited networks, regardless of encryption.

3. PHYSICAL TRANSMISSION

3.1 CONFIDENTIAL Material
CONFIDENTIAL material may be transmitted via:
    a) U.S. Postal Service Registered Mail (within CONUS)
    b) Authorized government courier
    c) Approved commercial carrier with chain-of-custody tracking

Material must be double-wrapped. The inner wrapper must be sealed, marked with
the classification level, and addressed to the intended recipient by name and
office. The outer wrapper must be unmarked and show no indication of classified
contents.

3.2 SECRET Material
SECRET material may be transmitted via:
    a) Defense Courier Service (DCS)
    b) Authorized government courier with SECRET clearance
    c) U.S. Postal Service Registered Mail (CONUS only, First Class)

The same double-wrapping procedures as CONFIDENTIAL apply. Additionally, a
receipt acknowledgment is required — the sender must receive confirmation of
delivery within 48 hours.

3.3 TOP SECRET Material
TOP SECRET material may only be transmitted via:
    a) Defense Courier Service (DCS)
    b) Specifically designated TOP SECRET courier with signed courier orders

Hand-to-hand transfer with signed receipt is required. Material must remain
under continuous positive control of a cleared individual at all times during
transit. TOP SECRET material may NOT be transmitted via U.S. mail under any
circumstances.

4. FACSIMILE (FAX) TRANSMISSION

4.1 STU-III or STE secure telephones with fax capability may be used for
transmitting information up to the SECRET level when both sending and
receiving terminals are secured.

4.2 TOP SECRET information may not be transmitted by facsimile.

4.3 All fax transmissions of classified material must be preceded by a test
page and confirmed by telephone with the receiving party.

5. VERBAL TRANSMISSION

5.1 Classified discussions must occur only in approved spaces — SCIFs for TOP
SECRET, and secured conference rooms certified for the appropriate level for
CONFIDENTIAL and SECRET.

5.2 The use of STU-III/STE secure telephones is authorized for classified
voice communications up to the level for which the equipment is keyed.

5.3 Discussion of classified information on unsecured telephones, cellular
phones, or in public spaces is strictly PROHIBITED.

6. ACCOUNTABILITY

6.1 All transmissions of SECRET and TOP SECRET information must be logged in
the division's classified material accountability system.

6.2 Receipts for TOP SECRET transmissions must be retained for five years.
Receipts for SECRET transmissions must be retained for two years.

6.3 Failure to obtain delivery confirmation within the required timeframe
must be reported as a potential security incident.
